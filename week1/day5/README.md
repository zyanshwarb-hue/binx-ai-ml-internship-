\## 🔍 What This Notebook Does



This is the capstone of Week 1 — every tool from the week (Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib)

combined into a single, reproducible analysis:



1\. \*\*Clean\*\* — Dropped `Cabin` (77% missing), imputed `Age` with the mean, dropped 2 rows missing `Embarked`

2\. \*\*Compute\*\* — Used `np.mean()` to calculate the overall survival rate

3\. \*\*Visualize\*\* — Built 3 fully labeled charts to explore the data



\---



\## 📊 Visualizations



| Chart | Question It Answers |

|---|---|

| 📈 Histogram | What did the age distribution of passengers look like? |

| 📊 Bar Chart | Did passenger class affect survival chances? |

| 🔵 Scatter Plot | Was there a relationship between age and ticket fare? |



\---



\## 💡 Key Insight



> First-class passengers had a noticeably higher survival count than second and third class —

> suggesting socioeconomic status played a real role in survival odds aboard the Titanic.

> Age and fare, however, showed no strong linear relationship.
